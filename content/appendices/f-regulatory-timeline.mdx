## Detailed Timeline

### 2013: FinCEN Virtual Currency Guidance

**March 2013** — FinCEN issued guidance clarifying that administrators and exchangers of virtual currency are money transmitters under the Bank Secrecy Act.

**Developer Impact:**
- Exchanges require money transmitter licenses
- Peer-to-peer applications may have compliance obligations
- Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements for custodial services

### 2014: IRS Cryptocurrency Taxation

**March 2014** — IRS Notice 2014-21 established that virtual currency is treated as property for federal tax purposes.

**Developer Impact:**
- Every crypto transaction is a taxable event
- Airdrops and rewards are taxable income
- Gas fees may be deductible as transaction costs

### 2017: The SEC DAO Report

**July 2017** — The SEC's Section 21(a) Report analyzed The DAO and concluded that DAO tokens were securities.

**Key Findings:**
- Tokens can be securities under the Howey test
- Decentralization doesn't automatically exempt tokens
- Functionality doesn't preclude security classification

**Developer Impact:**
- Token launches require securities law analysis
- "Utility tokens" may still be securities
- US persons may be excluded from token sales

### 2017: Global ICO Crackdown

**September 2017** — China banned ICOs outright; South Korea followed with restrictions.

**Developer Impact:**
- Geographic restrictions on token sales
- Rise of SAFT (Simple Agreement for Future Tokens)
- Increased use of offshore entities

### 2018: Congressional Hearings

**February 2018** — SEC and CFTC testified before Senate Banking Committee.

**Key Clarifications:**
- Bitcoin and Ether are not securities (per SEC Chair Clayton)
- CFTC has jurisdiction over crypto commodities
- ICO tokens require case-by-case analysis

### 2020: OCC Crypto Custody

**July 2020** — OCC clarified that national banks can provide cryptocurrency custody services.

**Developer Impact:**
- Institutional custody solutions expand
- Traditional finance integration accelerates
- Regulated on-ramps become more available

### 2021: Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act

**November 2021** — Passed with cryptocurrency broker reporting requirements.

**Key Provisions:**
- Expanded definition of "broker" for tax reporting
- $10,000+ crypto transaction reporting to IRS
- Effective January 2024 (reporting in 2025)

**Developer Impact:**
- DeFi protocol builder liability questions
- Validator and miner reporting requirements debated
- Potential privacy implications for users

### 2022: Tornado Cash Sanctions

**August 8, 2022** — OFAC sanctioned Tornado Cash addresses, marking the first time the US sanctioned a smart contract.

**Key Facts:**
- 45 Ethereum addresses added to SDN list
- Developer Alexey Pertsev arrested in Netherlands
- Roman Storm arrested in US (August 2023)

**Developer Impact:**
- Smart contract immutability meets sanctions law
- Frontend operators face compliance decisions
- Open source development liability questions raised
- Many DeFi protocols implemented address screening

### 2023: SEC Enforcement Actions

**June 2023** — SEC filed lawsuits against both Coinbase and Binance.

**Allegations:**
- Operating unregistered securities exchanges
- Offering unregistered securities (multiple tokens named)
- Staking services as securities

**Tokens Named as Securities:**
- SOL, ADA, MATIC, FIL, SAND, AXS, CHZ, FLOW, ICP, NEAR, VGX, DASH, NEXO

**Developer Impact:**
- Token classification uncertainty continues
- Staking service design considerations
- US market access decisions for projects

### 2024: MiCA Implementation

**June 30, 2024** — MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets) stablecoin provisions effective.
**December 30, 2024** — Full MiCA implementation.

**Key Requirements:**
- Crypto-Asset Service Provider (CASP) authorization
- Stablecoin issuer reserve requirements
- Whitepaper disclosure requirements
- Market abuse rules

**Developer Impact:**
- EU market requires CASP authorization
- Stablecoin integration considerations (USDC/EURC compliant, USDT uncertain)
- Consumer protection disclosures required

### 2024: Spot ETF Approvals

**January 10, 2024** — SEC approved 11 spot Bitcoin ETFs.
**July 23, 2024** — SEC approved spot Ethereum ETFs.

**Market Impact:**
- $50B+ flowed into Bitcoin ETFs in first year
- ETH ETFs approved with staking excluded
- Institutional allocation to crypto normalized

**Developer Impact:**
- Increased institutional interest in EVM development
- Regulatory clarity on BTC/ETH commodity status
- Enterprise blockchain adoption accelerates

### 2025: GENIUS Act

**Status:** Progressing through US Congress

**Key Provisions:**
- Federal and state licensing paths for stablecoin issuers
- Reserve requirements (1:1 backing, approved assets)
- Disclosure and audit requirements
- Consumer protection standards

**Developer Impact:**
- Regulatory clarity for stablecoin integration
- Compliant stablecoins identified (USDC, EURC likely qualifying)
- Algorithmic stablecoin restrictions

## Developer Compliance Considerations

### What Developers Should Know

1. **Token Classification** — Understand whether your token might be a security
2. **Geographic Restrictions** — Know which jurisdictions you can serve
3. **Smart Contract Liability** — Consider frontend and deployment decisions
4. **Sanctions Compliance** — Implement address screening where appropriate
5. **Tax Implications** — Understand reporting obligations for users

### Questions to Ask

- Is this token a security under the Howey test?
- Do we need money transmitter licenses?
- How do we handle OFAC-sanctioned addresses?
- What disclosures do MiCA require?
- How do we comply with tax reporting?

2. **Seek Counsel** — Consult legal professionals for specific guidance
3. **Build Flexibility** — Design systems that can adapt to changing requirements
4. **Document Decisions** — Record compliance considerations and rationale
