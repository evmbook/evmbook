# Era 9: 2021 — L2s, NFTs & Institutional Arrival

## Overview

2021 transformed cryptocurrency from a fringe technology to a mainstream financial phenomenon. NFTs achieved cultural visibility impossible to ignore. Layer 2 solutions launched on Ethereum mainnet, promising scaling without compromising decentralization. EIP-1559 reformed Ethereum's fee mechanism, introducing ETH burning. And for the first time, major institutions—Tesla, MicroStrategy, institutional funds—allocated treasury to Bitcoin. The "cycle" that began with DeFi Summer entered its speculative apex.

## EIP-1559: London Hard Fork

### The Problem

Ethereum's first-price auction for gas was:
- **Unpredictable**: Users guessed gas prices
- **Inefficient**: Users overpaid to ensure inclusion
- **Spiky**: Small demand increases caused huge price jumps

### The Solution (August 5, 2021)

EIP-1559 introduced:

```
Transaction fee = (Base fee + Priority fee) × Gas used
```

**Key changes**:
1. **Base fee**: Protocol-set, adjusts based on block fullness
2. **Priority fee** (tip): User incentive for miners
3. **Elastic blocks**: Target 15M gas, max 30M gas
4. **Base fee burned**: Deflationary pressure on ETH

[@eip_buterin_1559_2019]

### Block Dynamics

```
If block > 50% full:
  Next base fee increases (up to 12.5%)
If block < 50% full:
  Next base fee decreases (up to 12.5%)
```

This creates predictable fee markets and dampens spikes.

### ETH Burning

The base fee is **burned** (destroyed), not paid to miners.

**Impact by end of 2021**:
- 1M+ ETH burned
- At high usage, burn rate exceeded issuance ("ultrasound money" meme)
- Net deflationary during peak activity

### User Experience Improvement

| Before EIP-1559 | After EIP-1559 |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Guess gas price | See base fee, add tip |
| Uncertain confirmation | Predictable within 1-2 blocks |
| Overpay common | Overpay rare |
| Failed transactions | Fewer failures |

### Miner Response

Miners initially opposed EIP-1559 (reduced revenue), but:
- Priority fees still provided income
- Network health benefited overall
- The Merge (2022) made the debate moot

## Uniswap V3: Concentrated Liquidity

### The Innovation (May 2021)

Uniswap V3 introduced **concentrated liquidity**: LPs choose price ranges.

**V2 (old)**:
```
Liquidity distributed across 0 → ∞ price range
Most liquidity unused at any given price
Capital efficiency: ~0.5%
```

**V3 (new)**:
```
LP specifies: "I'll provide liquidity between $1500-$2000 ETH"
Liquidity concentrated where trading occurs
Capital efficiency: Up to 4000x improvement
```

[@whitepaper_uniswap_v3_2021]

### How It Works

LPs create "positions" within price ranges:

| Price Range | Liquidity Depth | LP Returns |
|-------------|-----------------|------------|
| Narrow (±1%) | Very deep | High if in range; 0 if out |
| Medium (±10%) | Moderate | Balanced risk/return |
| Wide (±50%) | Shallow | Lower but more consistent |

### Trade-offs

| Benefit | Cost |
|---------|------|
| Higher capital efficiency | Active management required |
| Better prices for traders | Impermanent loss more acute |
| Customizable strategies | Complexity for passive LPs |
| NFT positions (unique) | No fungible LP tokens |

### Market Impact

- Uniswap V3 became highest-volume DEX
- Spawned "LP management" protocols (Arrakis, Gamma)
- Influenced subsequent AMM designs

## El Salvador: First Nation-State Adoption

### Bitcoin Law (September 7, 2021)

El Salvador became the first country to adopt Bitcoin as legal tender.

**Key provisions**:
- Bitcoin legal tender alongside USD
- $30 BTC bonus for Chivo wallet users
- Government Bitcoin purchases
- Volcano mining initiative announced

**See**: [International Adoption](../../primitives/international_adoption.md) for detailed analysis.

### Global Reaction

- **IMF**: Criticized the move; conditioned later loan
- **World Bank**: Refused assistance for implementation
- **Crypto community**: Celebrated; tourism increased
- **Skeptics**: Questioned technical readiness

## Common Misattributions

### 1. "NFTs are just JPEGs"

**Reality**: NFTs are on-chain provenance records. The image is metadata; the token represents verified ownership and transfer history.

**Confidence**: HIGH

### 2. "EIP-1559 reduces gas prices"

**Reality**: EIP-1559 makes gas prices more **predictable**, not necessarily lower. Prices still rise with demand. The improvement is in UX, not cost.

**Confidence**: HIGH

### 3. "L2s are separate chains"

**Reality**: Rollups inherit Ethereum security by posting data/proofs to L1. They're extensions of Ethereum, not independent chains.

**Confidence**: HIGH
